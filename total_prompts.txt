Revenue Growth (%):
Revenue Growth (%) measures the increase (or decrease) in a company's revenue over a specific period, usually compared year-over-year (YoY) or quarter-over-quarter (QoQ). It indicates how fast a company is expanding its sales and is a key financial performance metric.
Formula for Revenue Growth (%)
Revenue Growth (%) = [(Current Period Revenue - Previous Period Revenue) / Previous Period Revenue] × 100
Example 
Let's assume a company's revenue for FY 2024 is ₹50 crore and for FY 2023 it was ₹40 crore.
Revenue Growth (%) = [(50 - 40) / 40] × 100
 Revenue Growth (%) = (10 / 40) × 100
 Revenue Growth (%) = 25%
Interpretation:
The company's revenue grew by 25% from FY 2023 to FY 2024, indicating strong business expansion.

EBITDA Margin (%)
EBITDA Margin (%) measures a company's profitability by showing how much of its revenue is converted into EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization). It helps assess operational efficiency and profitability before considering financial and accounting expenses.
Formula for EBITDA Margin (%)
EBITDA Margin (%) = (EBITDA / Total Revenue) × 100
Example 
Let's assume a company has:
Total Revenue for FY 2024 = ₹100 crore
EBITDA for FY 2024 = ₹25 crore
EBITDA Margin (%) = (25 / 100) × 100
 EBITDA Margin (%) = 25%
Interpretation:
The company has an EBITDA Margin of 25%, meaning it earns ₹25 in EBITDA for every ₹100 of revenue, indicating good operational profitability.
